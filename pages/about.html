So what is this?

This is a very personal project. It's regarding my closet and my style and the things I want, need and already have. I have always had an interest in clothing and textiles, more so than an interest in fashion and trends. This website serves two functions: firstly, to create a visual, structured list of what I need to buy, make and mend, and secondly to practice on creating websites.

I have a lot of thoughts related to style and clothing and I need somewhere to sort it out, so I might add some type of blog typ section here. A few years back I started a company where I focused on creating sustainable fashion in small quantities, mostly unique objects with hand embroidery, painted patterns and plant dyed fabrics. Then I ran head-first in to the wall, as we say in Sweden. I worked, studied full time and ran a company on the side. One day I woke up and was unable to get out of bed. Months of pushing through the tiredness, the anxiety and ignoring all types of signs that I wasn't doing well had caught up with me. It has taken me years to get back to a place where I feel good about myself and my situation. During these years I kind of lost a lot of myself and my creativity.

So this project is a way of getting back in to it. To structure my thoughts and my feelings and all of the things that are related to clothing and style. And there is a lot, from expressing yourself to environmental issues to working conditions in the fashion industry.

I have been thinking a lot about sustainability and how to be creative and keep a sense of style within the boundaries of sustainability. I've been seeing a lot of capsule wardrobes lately, but oftentimes they still seem to function around consumtion and seasonal changes.

I have no interest in seasonal changes, nor have I any interest in consumtion. I am looking to have a closet that is functional for me and the life I live, with pieces that can be used throughout the year and be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. This is something that I have been thinking about for quite some time and it is one if my most used and updated spreads in my bullet journal. This is a work in progress and could be understood in a plethora of ways, as a to do list, a moodboard, a personal style journal.
